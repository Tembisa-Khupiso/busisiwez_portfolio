My name is Busisiwe Mhlotshazana. I am a 24 year old, born and bred in Johannesburg, South Africa. I am the first born of three and there's a "thing" first born kids being "DEPUTY PARENTS" and I can say that I can relate. Being the first born has taught me a lot about responsibility and leadership. There are many things I value,family being one of them. I was raised by both my parents and even though there were ups and downs, they managed to raise my siblings and I in a warm and loving environment.


I attended primary school at Rosettenville Primary School. I was a bubbly child growing up and I made friends easily. I was part of the netball team from grade 1 to grade 7. I was also part of the Eistoddfod team and that is where i gained my experience and love for public speaking.Kenilworth High School, formally known as Kenilworth Junior Secondary School, is where I joined the debate team and grew in my public speaking skill. I then went to Hoerskool Die Fakkel where I completed the rest of my high school grades because Kenilworth Junior Secondary School only had Grades 8 and 9. The transition from a small school to a bigger one was unusual for me at first. For two years, I was in this small comfortable cocoon and had to go to a place where there are three times as many people as im used to seeing. I eventually adapted to this new environment, I did a bit of debate and netball.


I say one of the most confusing times in someone's life is the year after matric, at least for me it was. I wanted to study law but life had other plans for me.
